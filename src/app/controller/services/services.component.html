REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS

This phase involves gathering and analyzing the needs and specifications for an industrial automation project. It includes understanding the objectives, constraints, and functionalities desired by the client or end-user. The outcome is a detailed requirements document that serves as a blueprint for the project.

Design and Documentation

In this stage, engineers and designers translate the requirements into a detailed design. This encompasses creating schematics, diagrams, and specifications for the automation system. Documentation involves documenting every aspect of the design, including components, interfaces, and operational procedures, ensuring clarity and accuracy throughout the project lifecycle.

Fabrication and Assembly

Once the design is finalized, fabrication and assembly begin. This involves procuring or manufacturing the necessary components and assembling them into the intended configuration. Precision and quality are crucial to ensure that the system functions as intended.

Erection and Commissioning

During this phase, the assembled automation system is installed, integrated, and tested at the client's site. Erection involves physically setting up the components, while commissioning involves configuring and fine-tuning the system to ensure it meets performance requirements. This phase concludes with the system being ready for operation.

Breakdown and Maintenance

After the system is operational, ongoing maintenance is essential to ensure its continued performance and reliability. This includes preventive maintenance to minimize the risk of breakdowns as well as reactive maintenance to address any unexpected failures promptly. Regular inspections, troubleshooting, and repairs are part of this ongoing process to keep the automation system running smoothly.
